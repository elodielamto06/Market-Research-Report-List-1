<p><p>The stainless steel ingots market comprises two primary types: hot strapping and cold strapping. Hot strapping involves the processing of ingots at elevated temperatures, allowing for easier manipulation and shaping, which is essential for large-scale production. Cold strapping, on the other hand, occurs at lower temperatures, resulting in tighter tolerances and improved surface finishes, making it suitable for precision applications. Both methods cater to various industrial needs, influencing the overall demand for stainless steel ingots in diverse sectors.</p></p>
