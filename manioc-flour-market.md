<p><p>The manioc flour market is categorized into two main types: sweet manioc flour and bitter manioc flour. Sweet manioc flour is derived from cultivars with low cyanogenic compounds, making it safer for consumption and suitable for baking and cooking. Bitter manioc flour, on the other hand, comes from high-cyanogenic varieties and requires thorough processing to remove toxins, often used in traditional dishes where flavor and texture are paramount. Both types cater to diverse culinary needs and preferences globally.</p></p>
