<p><p>The Freshness Valve market is divided into two primary types: external and internal freshness valves. External freshness valves are designed to maintain product integrity by regulating air and moisture exchange externally, often used in packaging applications. Internal freshness valves, on the other hand, are integrated within the product or its container, allowing for controlled gas release and preventing spoilage from within. Both types play crucial roles in extending shelf life and preserving the quality of perishable goods across various industries.</p></p>
