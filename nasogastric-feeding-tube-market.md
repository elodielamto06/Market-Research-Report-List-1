<p><p>The nasogastric feeding tube market features several key types, including the Levin catheter, Salem Sump catheter, and Dobhoff tube. The Levin catheter is a single-lumen tube used for feeding and medication administration. The Salem Sump catheter is a double-lumen tube designed for gastric suction and feeding, offering greater flexibility and reduced risk of clogging. The Dobhoff tube is a smaller, softer option used primarily for enteral feeding, facilitating longer-term use and enhanced patient comfort, especially in critical care settings.</p></p>
