<p><strong>The Implant Intraocular Lens Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Non-foldable Lenses</li><li>Foldable Intraocular Lens</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The intraocular lens (IOL) market is primarily categorized into non-foldable lenses and foldable lenses. Non-foldable lenses are rigid, made from materials like polymethyl methacrylate (PMMA), and are typically implanted through larger incisions during cataract surgery. They offer durability but may require more recovery time. In contrast, foldable intraocular lenses are constructed from flexible materials, allowing them to be compressed for insertion through smaller incisions, resulting in quicker recovery and less trauma to the eye. Both types serve to restore vision post-surgery.</p></p>

<p><strong>The Implant Intraocular Lens Market Industry Research by Application is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Hydrophilic</li><li>Hydrophobic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Implant Intraocular Lens (IOL) market includes hydrophilic and hydrophobic lenses, each serving distinct applications in cataract surgery. Hydrophilic IOLs, made from water-loving materials, facilitate easier handling and typically exhibit less glistenings but may absorb water over time. In contrast, hydrophobic IOLs, which repel water, offer enhanced optical clarity and reduced risk of posterior capsule opacification. Both technologies are critical for optimizing patient outcomes post-surgery, catering to varying surgeon preferences and specific patient needs.</p></p>
