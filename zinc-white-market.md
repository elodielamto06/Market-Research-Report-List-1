<p><p>Zinc white, a pigment used predominantly in paints and coatings, is produced through various methods, each impacting its market dynamics. The Direct Process (American Method) rapidly oxidizes zinc metal, providing cost efficiency, while the Indirect Process (French Method) involves vaporizing zinc and recondensing it, resulting in finer particles with high purity, appealing to premium markets. The Wet Chemical Process, utilizing zinc salts, allows for controlled particle size and composition, catering to niche applications. Each method influences pricing, quality, and end-user preferences in the zinc white market.</p></p>
