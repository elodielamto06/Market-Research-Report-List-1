<p><p>The Guillain-Barre Syndrome treatment market primarily comprises two types: immunoglobulin therapy and plasma exchange (plasmapheresis). Immunoglobulin therapy involves administering high doses of intravenous immunoglobulins to modulate the immune system and reduce the damaging response. Plasma exchange, on the other hand, involves removing and replacing blood plasma to eliminate harmful antibodies from circulation. Both therapies aim to reduce symptoms, shorten recovery time, and improve patient outcomes, playing a crucial role in the management of this rare neurological disorder.</p></p>
